Private jets have become one of the clearest symbols of modern inequality: a tiny elite soaring above the world while the rest of us absorb the costs. Each hour of private flight emits more carbon than many people produce in an entire year. Yet these flights are increasing, often subsidized through loopholes and tax breaks. What does it mean when luxury travel becomes a collective burden?

Your yearly CO₂ emissions (~13.8 metric tons) are extremely small compared to private jet emissions, which often exceed 1,000 metric tons annually.

Climate action has been framed as an individual responsibility: buy reusable bags, eat less meat, take shorter showers. But when a single private jet flight emits as much CO2 as the average person produces in a year, we're not all in the same boat (some of us are in yachts).
